B. Tennessee Law on Total Loss Vehicles and Title Issues (TN Code ¬ß 55-3-120(c), ¬ß 55-3-209)

Upon a total loss declaration, the original Certificate of Title is void. The insurer obtaining the vehicle *shall* obtain a salvage title. Progressive's demand for Mr. Herd to produce the original title or a bonded title *after* taking possession and (initially) declaring it a total loss is problematic and appears to misapply or ignore their statutory obligations.

C. Insurer's Obligations Regarding Rental Car Coverage and Claim Delays

Rental coverage typically extends until settlement, subject to policy limits. However, if insurer's unreasonable delays cause rental to exceed limits, it can be argued the insurer should bear additional costs, especially given principles of good faith and prohibitions against unreasonable delays.

D. "Bad Faith" in Insurance Claims under Tennessee Law (TN Code ¬ß 56-7-105)

An insurer can be found in "bad faith" for refusing to pay a claim without a legitimate basis or failing to respond to a formal demand within 60 days. Proof requires a valid claim, formal demand, and bad faith refusal. Penalties can be up to 25% of the claim amount. The Unfair Claims Settlement Practices Act guides courts in determining bad faith.

E. Tennessee Unfair Trade Practices Act (TN Code ¬ß 56-8-104)

This Act prohibits unfair or deceptive acts in insurance, including misrepresenting policy terms or conditions. Contradictory advice on bonded titles or issues with initial underwriting versus later title disputes could fall under this Act.

F. Policyholder's Rights Despite Not Having Physical Policy Document

Mr. Herd's insurance ID card, premium payments, and claim correspondence provide substantial evidence of an existing contract. Insurers are obligated to provide policy copies on request. The claim should be adjudicated based on established coverage.
