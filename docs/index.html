Tennessee Legal Framework

The dispute over vehicle ownership is governed by specific state laws. This section outlines the relevant Tennessee Code Annotated (TCA) statutes that define the process for an insurer acquiring a salvage vehicle.

Insurer's Duty in Acquiring a Salvage Vehicle

Contrary to the claim representative's statement that he "could not locate" a relevant statute, Tennessee law is explicit. The law not only allows for an insurer to take ownership but describes it as a standard part of the process.

TCA ยง 55-3-212(b)(8)(B)(i)

"If an insurance company, pursuant to a damage settlement, acquires ownership of a passenger motor vehicle that has incurred damage requiring the vehicle to be titled as a salvage vehicle... the insurance company... must... apply for a salvage title... within fifteen (15) days after the title is properly assigned by the owner to the insurance company..."

Implication: The law clearly uses the term "acquires ownership," directly supporting the policyholder's position. Progressive's action of taking possession of the vehicle strongly implies the initiation of this acquisition process, making their denial of ownership a direct contradiction of both their actions and this statutory framework.
